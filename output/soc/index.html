SOC

Service Organization Control

Description

Service Organization Control (SOC) refers to a set of standards established by the American Institute of Certified Public Accountants (AICPA) that are designed to help service organizations manage their data and protect the interests of their clients. In the context of fraud decisioning, SOC reports are crucial as they provide assurance regarding the effectiveness of an organization's internal controls, particularly those that relate to data protection and fraud prevention. There are various types of SOC reports, including SOC 1, SOC 2, and SOC 3, each catering to different aspects of service delivery and risk management. For organizations involved in fraud decisioning, a SOC 2 report is particularly relevant, as it evaluates the controls related to security, availability, processing integrity, confidentiality, and privacy. This helps businesses ensure that their service providers are managing data securely and are committed to preventing fraudulent activities. A reliable SOC report can enhance trust and transparency between service providers and their clients, thereby strengthening partnerships and compliance with regulatory requirements.

Examples

  • A financial institution requiring a SOC 2 report from its data analytics provider to ensure data security controls are in place.
  • An e-commerce platform seeking a SOC 1 report from its payment processor to verify controls over financial reporting.

Additional Information

  • SOC reports are often used in vendor risk management processes.
  • Obtaining a SOC report can provide a competitive advantage in the marketplace.
